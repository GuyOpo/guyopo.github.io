01  /  Pipeline Generation

Outbound works when
the system works.

Most teams skip the system and wonder why the numbers don't move.

Bad data kills connect rate before anyone dials. Messaging that leads with your product instead of their pain kills conversion before they hear a word. Most outbound has both problems at once.

The fix is not more dials. It is fixing what breaks the dials you are already making.

LinkedIn comes first because warm beats cold. The sequence adapts at touch 2 based on whether the connection was accepted. By touch 10 you have earned the right to break the pattern.

Touch 1Day 1LinkedIn connection invite
Touch 2Day 3LinkedIn video (accepted) / InMail (not accepted)
Touch 3Day 4Call — following up on LinkedIn outreach
Touch 4Day 4Follow-up email
Touch 5Day 6Phone call
Touch 6Day 8LinkedIn InMail
Touch 7Day 10Phone call
Touch 8Day 12Event or webinar invite email
Touch 9Day 13LinkedIn profile view
Touch 10Day 15Personalized direct mail
Touch 11Day 17Customer proof point email + call with voicemail
Touch 12Day 19Value add email
Touch 13Day 21Call with voicemail
Touch 14Day 23Personalized InMail
Touch 15Day 25Call with voicemail

The profile view at touch 9 is deliberate. It signals presence without making a request. The physical drop at touch 10 works because by then the prospect has seen your name enough times to recognize it on an envelope.

LinkedIn

Start here, not after the cold call. A short personalized video stands out in a feed full of text.

Email

Subject line: 2-3 words about their pain, not your product. Open with their world. The first CTA is a question that finds out whether the prospect shares the pain you described. You are qualifying interest before requesting time.

Cold Call

The best opener is the one you can deliver with genuine confidence. One of my BDRs opened every call with "What did I catch you in the middle of?" Completely sincere. She never got hung up on. When other reps tried the same line it fell flat because it was not them. I coach reps to find their open, not memorize mine.

Voicemail

Getting a callback from a cold voicemail is nearly impossible. So do not ask for one. Use the voicemail as a delivery mechanism instead. Point the prospect toward something valuable.

Not all signals carry the same weight. Someone who visits the demo page is further along than someone who clicked one link in an email. The scoring should reflect that difference.

I build signal weighting collaboratively with Marketing and RevOps so the thresholds are agreed upon, not imposed. When everyone works from the same definition of a high-intent account, the targeting improves and handoffs get cleaner.

ABX at its best is a decision to treat a specific person like a specific person. That starts with research. Not just the company and the pain, but the individual.

At one of my companies we were trying to reach a specific contact at a major financial institution. Calls went unanswered. Emails unopened. LinkedIn messages ignored. A little research revealed he was an avid fisherman. We worked with marketing to send a custom mailer with a fishing lure with a pendant inscribed: "Ron, I've been trying to catch you." He booked a meeting the next day.

You cannot do that for every prospect. But the principle scales: find the human detail, build the touch around it, send something that could only have been made for that one person. The research is the work. The lure was just the delivery.

Bad data is a targeting problem. Most teams treat it as maintenance and wonder why connect rates stay low.

Clean data is a competitive advantage. When it improves, connect rate rises before you change a single thing about messaging or cadence. SDRs putting accurate qualification notes, clean company data, and updated opportunity stages into the system is what makes reporting reliable and leadership visibility real.

02  /  The AI-Native Stack

AI should be a value add
for SDRs.

Many reps spend a significant part of their day on tasks that could be automated. AI removes the work that keeps reps from making more dials and having great conversations.

Enrichment tools (Clay, Apollo, ZoomInfo) pull data from multiple sources and assemble a complete picture of a prospect and account before a rep ever opens a tab.

Job postings that signal a relevant initiative. Tech stack changes at a target account. Leadership changes that create buying windows. Intent signals weighted by action type. The output is a prioritized, enriched contact list where the targeting decisions have already been made. Reps work the list. They do not build it.

Getting this right requires a close partnership with RevOps on data flows, field mapping, and workflow logic. I have built this from scratch and I have inherited broken versions of it. The difference is almost always in that partnership.

Auto dialers (Nooks, Orum, ConnectAndSell) change two things: call volume and real-time coaching.

On volume: reps connect with more prospects per session than sequential dialing allows. That matters when you need more conversations to find the ones worth having.

Being on the phones with the team during live call blocks lets me give direction in the moment. That lands differently than a debrief an hour later. I run structured call blocks with the team. It is one of the fastest ways to move a struggling rep forward quickly.

The tools only work when they connect.

Enrichment tools handle enrichment and signal scoring. Data providers (Apollo, ZoomInfo) supply contact and account data. Sales intelligence platforms (LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Lusha) support research and warm outreach. Sequencing platforms (Outreach, Salesloft) manage execution. Call recording and analytics (Gong, Chorus) capture every call for coaching and analysis.

A stack where data does not move cleanly between systems creates manual work that defeats the purpose of having the stack at all. I work closely with RevOps on the integration layer.

AI cannot be genuinely curious about a prospect's business. It cannot deliver a cold call open with authentic confidence. It cannot build the kind of trust that makes a stranger want to keep talking.

Curiosity is one of my four non-negotiables in hiring because no tool replaces a rep who is genuinely interested in the person on the other end of the phone.

03  /  Talent

Skills can be taught.
Traits cannot.

Before I look at a resume I want to know whether someone has four things.

Drive

Internal fire. Sets big goals and chases them in work and in life. Not about being loud. About what someone does when nobody is watching.

Resilience

This job is rejection. Most calls do not connect. I need people who see setbacks as data, not as failure.

Curiosity

Genuine interest in the customer's business, the market, and the product. Curious people become trusted advisors.

Charisma

Someone you would want to have a cup of coffee with. Can they make a stranger want to keep talking? That instinct cannot be trained.

If someone has all four I can teach them everything else.

The Rising Performer

Hunger to learn before hunger to close. Obsessive about process. Top 20% within six months is the benchmark I look for.

The Proven Closer

Three to five years quota-carrying. Navigates complex organizations and multi-threads pipeline. Runs MEDDPICC without being prompted.

The Career Changer

Zero sales background but elite transferable skills. No bad habits to unlearn. The profile that surprises people most. The ones I have hired have consistently been among my best performers.

I am not testing for polish in an interview. I am looking for the four traits, plus two more.

Drive surfaces in how someone talks about goals outside of work. Resilience surfaces in how they talk about their hardest stretch. Curiosity surfaces in the questions they ask. Charisma surfaces in the first thirty seconds.

Coachability

I test this in the interview itself. I give real-time feedback during the roleplay and watch how they respond. Someone who adjusts quickly and without defensiveness is showing me something I cannot teach.

Cultural Add

I want people who bring new energy and perspective. Everyone wants to be number one, but never at a peer's expense.

Nobody goes live until they have passed certification at every stage. Five tracks run simultaneously: Subject Matter Expert, Core BDR Skills, Methodology (MEDDPICC, Command of the Message), The Handbook, and Mock Calls with Certification. The prospect deserves a prepared rep. The rep deserves to know they are ready.

At every company I have led, I built a record of promoting people into AE, Marketing, RevOps, team lead, and management roles. At Alteryx, ten BDRs promoted into sales roles. At BigPanda, multiple into AE, RevOps, Marketing, and management. At Chronosphere, multiple promoted to AE.

Enable all BDRs on AE skills from day one. For high performers on a clear path to promotion, go deeper. When you have a visible next step, you attract and keep the best talent.

I do not want culture fit. I want culture add. Someone who brings something the team does not already have. BDR Summits quarterly, Book Clubs monthly, Peer Mentorship, Spiffs and Competition. The goal is a team that lifts each other up, not a group of individuals hitting their own number.

04  /  Coaching

I coach from inside
the work.

Not from a dashboard. From the phones, from call blocks, from the 1:1 where the rep owns the agenda.

I am on the phones with the team, in call blocks, in office hours, in private call hours when someone needs it. The feedback I give is connected to real work happening in real time.

Coaching starts before the first live call. New reps go through mock calls with certification at every stage. You do not go live with a real prospect until you have passed. The prospect deserves a prepared rep. The rep deserves to know they are ready.

I flip the traditional 1:1. The rep owns the agenda. What they are struggling with. Questions they do not feel comfortable asking in a group setting. A plan for the week, month, or quarter that we check in on together.

My job in that meeting is to figure out how I can help them. A manager staring at a dashboard and deciding someone needs to make more calls is a waste of both people's time.

I always start at the top of the funnel. Are they making enough calls and sending enough outreach? If yes, I go to quality. Are the calls landing? Is the messaging working?

From there we build a plan together. Clear leading indicators. Real checkpoints. They always know where they stand and what needs to change. No surprises. Ever. My intention is always to help someone come out of a difficult stretch, not to document their way out.

Call recording and analytics (Gong, Chorus) show you what happened. Auto dialers let you coach while it is happening. Both matter and serve different purposes.

Recorded calls are where I do pattern work. I listen for how reps handle the moment after an objection and whether their delivery feels natural or rehearsed.

Being on the phones with the team during call blocks is where I see reps move fastest. The best cold call open is the one a rep can deliver with genuine confidence. My job is helping each rep find theirs.

05  /  The Operating Model

Leadership should never
have to ask.

The three metrics I watch most closely week to week.

Connect to Meeting

The clearest gap in most outbound funnels. The most coachable metric on the board. When this number moves, everything downstream moves.

No-Show Rate

A high no-show rate is a qualification problem dressed up as a scheduling problem. Tells me whether we are booking meetings people genuinely want to attend.

SAO Acceptance

AE-validated and tied directly to pipeline quality. The metric that connects SDR activity to revenue in a way leadership can trust.

Weekly

1:1s with each BDR (rep owns the agenda). Team meeting (only what cannot be answered by email). BDR Manager check-in. Pipeline review with leadership.

Bi-Weekly

BDR 1:1s for coaching, development, and career progression. Office hours: open drop-in, no agenda, just come.

Structured but never bureaucratic. The goal of every meeting is to be useful.

Every week I send a short update to leadership. The numbers. What is improving. Where I see risk. What I am doing about it. That visibility keeps everyone aligned and prevents surprises later.

Connect to Meeting and No-Show Rate are the weekly signals that let me spot problems early. SAO is more of a lagging indicator given longer enterprise sales cycles.

RevOps is a force multiplier when the relationship works both ways. My job is to make their lives easier. Clean data. Processes followed consistently. Issues surfaced early. When I bring a concern I bring context and a point of view, not just a complaint.

My team and marketing work from the same calendar. If there is a webinar, event, or content push coming, the SDR team already knows about it and is using it in outreach before it launches. The best campaigns wrap SDR activity around marketing moments rather than running parallel to them.

I prefer direct and transparent communication across every relationship. I would rather surface a question or a problem early and adjust quickly than let something drift. The SDR org does not exist in isolation. How well it works is largely determined by how well those relationships work.

06  /  The First 90 Days

I would not come in
with decisions.

I would come in with questions. Click each phase to expand.

→
30

Diagnose

Days 1 to 30

Stakeholder interviews across every function that touches the SDR org. Shadow everything: calls, 1:1s, team meetings, pipeline reviews. Assess the tech stack, data quality, sequence structure, and reporting.

By day 30 I present to leadership one clear answer: what is the single biggest bottleneck in this organization right now. Everything else I want to see firsthand before I touch it.

→
60

Repair

Days 31 to 60

Fix the number one bottleneck. Deploy what I know works and adapt it to what I learned in the first 30 days. Updated sequences and cadences. Cross-functional feedback loops with RevOps and Marketing. Enablement frameworks introduced.

By day 60 early results should be visible: higher conversion, cleaner pipeline, frameworks in motion.

→
90

Build

Days 61 to 90

Scale what is working. Development programs launched. A hiring pipeline built around the profiles I have defined. Clear promotion paths documented. OKRs set with leadership aligned on targets.

By day 90 I deliver a State of the BDR Org report: where we started, what changed, what the org looks like now, and what we are building toward.
